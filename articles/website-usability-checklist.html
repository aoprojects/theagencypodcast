Usability Checklist for Websites and Digital Products

Designing a usable product means that users will return to your product (assuming that they are aware enough of you) the next time they need whatever it is that you provide.

So decide to be usable now. It's easier than fixing a bad design later.

  1. What did the user come to your site wondering?
  2. Did they get the answer to that question in 3 seconds or less?
  3. What do they want to do next, now that they have that answer?
  4. Is that action ^ the next most available thing they can do on your site?
  5. Can they answer "WTF is this company?" on the first page of the mobile version, without scrolling or pinching?
  6. Can they contact you quickly?
  7. Can they complain to you quickly?
  8. Can they find out when you're open for business - hours?
  9. Can they find out how much you charge for your services?
  10. Can they see pictures of your physical space, if you have one? (they want to figure out what the experience of interacting with your business will feel like)
  11. Can they begin to interact with you, then get busy, then save their work, do something else now instead, and later come back to exactly where the left off?
  12. Will you remind them to come back later, if they pause their work before clicking "submit"?
  13. Do all your links work on mobile - phone numbers actually click to initiate a call or a text, and emails link to initiate a new email draft?
  14. Is your logo readable from far away and in 1 second?
  15. Can all areas of your website be read without pinching?
  16. Did they somehow ask or imply direct desire for the information you're displaying? If not, hide it. They're not ready for it. Keep it simple.
  17. Can they quickly sign up for your mailing list? They might want to leave and come back later. A mailing list email can be the thing that reminds them to come back later.
  18. Are there enough visuals on your site such that they can figure out what's going on without really reading it? They don't trust your content yet, and they won't spend time reading it before they trust it.
  19. If they need to sign in, is it easy to do on mobile?
  20. Is your goal of them very VERY clear and easy for them to decide to do immediately?

Thanks for reading.
